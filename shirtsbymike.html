Objective
The current site by ShirtsbyMike is outdated and needs to be revamped with a layout and design that intrigues customers to buy their t-shirts.
ShirtsbyMike aims to sell t-shirts that are fun and hopes to use their product as a way to bring a connect community amongst Treehouse students. Their goal is to increase t-shirt sales by providing a limited edition t-shirt of the month.

With my redesign, I aimed to not only increase t-shirt sales by emphasizing the featured t-shirt on various sections of the site, but also aimed to attract Treehouse students with the new branding in color and features focusing on the community.

Collecting all my findings, and understanding the companyâ€™s goals and the user pain points, I was then able to wireframe the first redesign, noting the essential components needed - Contact Page, media sections, etc. With wireframes, I was able to focus on the user experience of the page and how each page functions. Outcome
As with any UX Design Project, I realized and learned that UX Design is not entirely linear. Throughout this entire process of redesigning the ShirtsbyMike site, I kept going back and forth through many of the steps to reaffirm by new insights and connect them back to the initial findings.

In addition to this new learning, I also learned that UX Design is not about solving the visual problems when it comes to the user experience but also the text content as well.
