About

Intrigued by the intersection of design, tech, and educational spaces, tied in with my personal experience of teaching myself and others, I'm interested in designing tools to create accessible and inclusive experiences, especially to those in underrepresented communities.

I'm currently earning my UX Design Techdegree at Treehouse and I'm a UX Design apprentice at Niantic.

Background
Previously, I studied Art History and Media at the University of California, Berkeley where I focused on the power and complexity of visual spaces. During my time at Cal, I worked as a graphic designer, illustrator, and design lead with a variety of student organizations that helped me jumpstart and hone my skills as a digital creator, a team spirit animal, and a helpful mentor.

Most of my professional experience resides in crafting digital UX design for web platforms, but I’ve explored alternative methods for UX research, VR set design, and graphic design. I’ve worked with early-stage startups as the product designer and graphic designer, with company values on self-improvement and fair compensation among underrepresented groups. Working with both remote and non-remote teams have shaped how I collaborate with teammates and think when solving problems that serve particular communities.
