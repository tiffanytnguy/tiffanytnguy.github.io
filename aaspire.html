Overview
When it comes to driving oneself to achieve self-improvement goals, there's a plethora of resources that can be found online. However, it's challenging to find which type of resource works best — quiz, text, online courses, etc., and to know whether the content is vetted by experts.

What did I do to solve the problem?
— Developed web application screens and additional features, aimed to provide a variety of content to help individuals achieve their self-improvement and learning goals and to offer creators a variety of ways to display their content.

— Applied a variety of content features to approve or disapprove current content, recommend new material, etc.

— Additional: Redesigned landing pages for both basic users and creators to make note of our product's value props. Created an invitation page, directed to recruit creators.

— Lead weekly design presentations with the engineering team to go over the design roadmap and ideation of new features and screens, which ensured a streamlined process.
Learnings
Some takeaways that I got during my internship...
— Equip a learning and career growth mindset through challenging comfort zones and proactively learning. Always ask team mates for another set of eyes on a project!

— Design one step ahead and offer alternative layouts, showcasing other screens and edge cases. This ensures that all ideas are documented and minimizes the time-length of developer-handoff.

— When it comes to presentations, lay out the user problem and user goals aligning with business vision before presenting final solution. Lay out the product as such: “I am showing [and early / a work-in-progress/ the final] solution around [problem] because [why] and am looking for feedback around [areas].” I found huge insight on handling feedback sessions with this design critique template and shared it with the team so they could offer helpful constructive feedback to every project — design, back-end, front-end.
